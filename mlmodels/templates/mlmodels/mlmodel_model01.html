Machine Learning Models

Ask:

Does the victim's age, gender, relationship to the person that recruited them and citizenship predict the type of exploitation?

Polaris-reported human trafficking appears to fall in to one of five major types of exploitation: sexual, forced labor, forced marriage, slavery and other/multiple exploits.

Data ETL

Categorical data was converted to numerical data using multiple loops and .replace cells within a Jupyter Notebook. This transformation was necessary to meet the requirements of our machine learning model. Null values were dropped as we could not presume to know things like country of origin or reasonably infer things like gender based on the information available.

Model Choice

Initial modeling attempts were made using both deep learning neural networks as well as unsupervised learning models to predict a categorical outcome. When those efforts resulted in less than desirable predictive models, consultations with our instructor led to the creation of a supervised, multi-class logistic regression model.

Model Performance

In predicting the type of exploitation, our chosen model performed well. Using SciKit-Learn, an accuracy score of .969 was calculated. Further examination using classification_report yielded the following:
